How we choose our picks

We don’t buy every fire pit or run lab tests. Instead, we spend weeks reading thousands of owner reviews on major retailer sites, forums like Reddit and Houzz, and expert roundups from trustworthy outdoor-gear publications. We look for patterns in long-term reliability, things that actually break (igniters, rust spots, warped grates) and what owners say after a season or two. Then we cross-reference with our own experience using dozens of pits over the years, plus manufacturer specs and warranty details. We prioritize models with a strong reputation across many sources, not just one glowing review. If a pit has widespread complaints about leaking gas valves or rusting through in a year, it doesn’t make the list, no matter how shiny it looks in the ad.
